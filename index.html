About

In a Full-Stack Flex program hosted at the University of Kansas and powered by Trilogy, the skills I'm learning, honing and building are in HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Java, Bootstrap, Express.js, React.js, Node.js, Database Theory, Bookshelf.js, MongoDB, MySQL, Command Line and Git.

My hobbies are my life and I love them tremendously. I adore knitting, sewing, baking, and, most of all, reading. My son and I have been involved in an astronomy club recently which has redefined awesome in our lives!

Professionally my career started in insurance regulation. In an assistant role to the Research and Actuarial Services at the National Association of Insurance Commissioners, I was able to discover and develop a myriad of skills. Beyond daily administrative tasks for a staff that supported over 40 committees and subcommittees, I jumped into up and coming topics that would potentially affect the insurance industry. I was introduced to web development on an administrative daily maintenance level and predictive data analytics on a executive and regulatory level.

Star Trekking

This was my first group project. With three of my classmates, we created the Star Trekking website. The user can navigate to our site and once arriving, the geolocation of their IP address will update a map, an image of the stars, a planetary chart and the local weather.

My responsibilites were adding an astronomy API that displays the current location of all planets in our solar system, generating a dynamic star chart image incorporating geolocation capturing from the IP address and initializing the Firebase database to capture and store information received from the contact page.
